About This Destination

Explore the Florida Keys by Yacht

Key West is the crown jewel of the Florida Keys and one of the most extraordinary yacht charter destinations in the world. Departing from Key West harbor, you will enter a realm of crystal-clear backcountry waters, uninhabited sandbar islands, and vibrant coral reefs teeming with marine life. The turquoise shallows of the Keys backcountry are so clear you can watch stingrays and sea turtles glide beneath the hull as you cruise.

Our Key West charters offer access to experiences you simply cannot find elsewhere. Anchor at a secluded sandbar where your group is the only soul for miles, snorkel pristine coral reef systems in the Florida Keys National Marine Sanctuary, and watch the legendary Mallory Square sunset celebration from the most exclusive vantage point — your own private yacht. For the truly adventurous, we can arrange a full-day voyage toward the Dry Tortugas, a remote national park 70 miles west of Key West that is home to Fort Jefferson and some of the most spectacular snorkeling in North America.

Key West Yacht FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, a Dry Tortugas day trip is one of our most exclusive offerings. The Dry Tortugas National Park is located approximately 70 miles west of Key West and is home to the magnificent Fort Jefferson, pristine beaches, and some of the clearest water in the Western Hemisphere. Due to the distance, this trip requires our 55' Sunseeker or 80' Princess and a full-day charter. Your captain will navigate through open water to reach Garden Key, where you can explore the fort, snorkel over coral gardens, and enjoy a level of seclusion that few visitors ever experience. We recommend booking this as an overnight charter for the most relaxed and rewarding experience.
Key West sits within the Florida Keys National Marine Sanctuary, home to the only living coral barrier reef in the continental United States. Our captains regularly anchor at top snorkeling sites including the reef off Sand Key, the shallow coral gardens near the Western Dry Rocks, and the patch reefs surrounding the Marquesas Keys. You can expect to see vibrant coral formations, tropical fish including parrotfish and angelfish, sea turtles, and nurse sharks. We provide premium snorkeling equipment on all charters at no additional charge, and our crew will guide you to the best spots based on current conditions and visibility.
Key West enjoys a tropical climate with warm temperatures year-round, making it an excellent charter destination in every season. The peak season from November through April offers the most comfortable conditions — warm days, low humidity, calm seas, and excellent visibility for snorkeling. Summer months bring warmer water temperatures and longer days, ideal for swimming and extended sunset cruises, though afternoon thunderstorms are common. Our captains adjust itineraries based on conditions to ensure you always have the best experience. We recommend booking well in advance for peak season, especially around the holidays and during Key West's festival calendar.

Yes, overnight charters are available on our 55' Sunseeker Predator and 80' Princess V78, both of which feature comfortable staterooms, full bathrooms, and climate-controlled sleeping quarters.
