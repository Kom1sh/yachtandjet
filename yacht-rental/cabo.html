About This Destination

Explore the Sea of Cortez from Land's End

Cabo San Lucas sits at the very tip of the Baja Peninsula, where the Pacific Ocean meets the Sea of Cortez — a body of water Jacques Cousteau famously called "the aquarium of the world." This unique convergence creates some of the most biodiverse waters on Earth, teeming with dolphins, sea lions, manta rays, and, during winter months, magnificent humpback and gray whales that migrate through these warm channels.

Your charter begins at the Cabo San Lucas marina and immediately takes you past the iconic El Arco — the dramatic natural stone arch at Land's End that has become the symbol of Los Cabos. From there, cruise to Lovers Beach, a secluded stretch of sand accessible only by water, or anchor in a protected cove for snorkeling in crystal-clear visibility. For sportfishing enthusiasts, Cabo is legendary — marlin, tuna, dorado, and wahoo make these waters a world-class angling destination year-round. Anchor off Medano Beach in the evening and watch the sunset paint the desert cliffs in shades of gold.

Cabo San Lucas Yacht FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Our yachts can cruise very close to El Arco for stunning photos and views of Land's End. The Arch is located at the very tip of the Baja Peninsula where the Pacific meets the Sea of Cortez, and our captains navigate these waters daily. Depending on sea conditions, we can position the yacht within a few hundred feet of the rock formations for panoramic views and excellent photo opportunities. We also pass by the nearby sea lion colony, Pelican Rock, and Neptune's Finger — all part of the iconic Land's End approach.
Absolutely. Cabo San Lucas is one of the world's premier sportfishing destinations, and fishing charters are among our most popular offerings. The waters off Cabo are home to striped marlin, blue marlin, yellowfin tuna, dorado (mahi-mahi), wahoo, and roosterfish. We can equip our yachts with professional-grade fishing tackle and arrange for a fishing mate to join the crew. The best fishing months are October through December for marlin, though excellent catches are made year-round. Mexican fishing licenses are required and can be arranged through our team before your charter.
Whale season in Cabo San Lucas runs from December through April. Humpback whales and gray whales migrate from the cold waters of Alaska and the Arctic to the warm, protected bays of Baja California to breed and nurse their calves. January through March is peak viewing season, with frequent sightings of breaching, tail-slapping, and mother-calf pairs. A private yacht charter offers the most comfortable and intimate whale-watching experience — far from the crowded tour boats — with your captain positioning you at safe, respectful distances for extended viewing.
Yes. Cabo San Lucas is located in Mexico, and all international travelers need a valid passport to enter the country. U.S. citizens need a passport book or passport card for air travel to Mexico. Your passport should be valid for at least six months beyond your travel dates. No visa is required for tourist stays under 180 days. Our team can provide guidance on travel logistics, including recommended flights, airport transfers, and hotel accommodations near the Cabo San Lucas marina.
Lovers Beach (Playa del Amor) is accessible only by water, making it a highlight of any Cabo yacht charter. On the Sea of Cortez side, the beach offers calm, swimmable water with excellent snorkeling among tropical fish and rocky formations. However, the opposite side — Divorce Beach — faces the open Pacific and has dangerous currents and waves that make swimming unsafe. Our captain will anchor on the protected Sea of Cortez side and can shuttle you to the beach via the yacht's tender. We recommend spending 30 to 60 minutes ashore before continuing your charter.
