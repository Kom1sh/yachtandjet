About This Destination

The Defining Experience of Hamptons Summer

The Hamptons have long been synonymous with understated luxury, and nothing captures that spirit quite like a private yacht charter along Long Island's South Shore. From the weathered beauty of the Montauk Lighthouse to the charming village docks of Sag Harbor, every nautical mile reveals another facet of this legendary coastal retreat. Our seasonal operation runs from May through October, aligning perfectly with the Hamptons social calendar.

Drop anchor off Shelter Island for a secluded afternoon swim, cruise past the grand estates of Meadow Lane in Southampton, or time your charter for the golden hour when the Long Island sky transforms into a canvas of amber and rose. Whether you are hosting an intimate gathering of close friends or celebrating a summer milestone, a Hamptons yacht charter is the experience that defines the season.

Hamptons Yacht FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Our Hamptons yacht charter season runs from May through October. Peak season is Memorial Day through Labor Day, when the Hamptons social calendar is at its busiest and water temperatures are warmest. Shoulder months (May and October) offer quieter waters, stunning fall foliage along the coastline, and easier booking availability. We recommend reserving peak-season weekends at least six to eight weeks in advance.
Yes, the Montauk run is one of our most popular charter routes. Departing from our Sag Harbor marina, you will cruise along the South Fork coastline past Amagansett and the Napeague stretch before arriving at Montauk Harbor. The journey takes approximately 90 minutes each way and includes views of the historic Montauk Lighthouse from the water. You can dock briefly in Montauk for lunch at the harbor restaurants before returning at sunset. Full-day charters are recommended for the Montauk route.
Absolutely. We partner with premier Hamptons caterers who specialize in yacht dining. Options range from artisanal charcuterie and raw bars to full multi-course meals prepared on board or delivered dock-side before departure. Rosé packages are a perennial favorite, and we can arrange champagne service, craft cocktail setups, and custom menus to match your event theme. Let us know your preferences at booking and we will coordinate every detail so you can simply enjoy the experience.
Sunset timing varies across the season. In late June and July, the sun sets around 8:20 PM, giving you the longest golden-hour window for evening charters. By September, sunset moves closer to 7:00 PM, which many guests actually prefer as the light takes on warmer, more dramatic tones. Our charter team will recommend the ideal departure time based on your date to ensure you are positioned in the perfect spot — typically off Shelter Island or the Montauk bluffs — when the sky puts on its show.
Hamptons weekends — especially during July and August — are our most in-demand time slots. We recommend booking six to eight weeks in advance for peak-season Saturdays and Sundays. Holiday weekends (Memorial Day, Fourth of July, Labor Day) often sell out two to three months ahead. Weekday charters are easier to secure on shorter notice and offer the same beautiful waters with fewer boats. Contact our team early to lock in your preferred date and yacht.
