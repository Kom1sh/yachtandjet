About This Destination

Cruise Biscayne Bay in Luxury

Miami is the yacht charter capital of the United States, and for good reason. From the turquoise waters of Biscayne Bay to the glittering Miami skyline, every moment on deck delivers an unforgettable spectacle. Glide past the mansions of Star Island where celebrities and billionaires call home, anchor at the famous Haulover sandbar for a lively afternoon on the water, and watch the sun dip behind the South Beach coastline as the city lights begin to shimmer.

Our Miami yacht charters include a licensed captain and full-service crew, so you can focus entirely on the experience. Whether you are planning a sunset cruise along the South Beach coastline, a corporate event on Biscayne Bay, or a celebration with friends at one of Miami's legendary sandbar parties, our fleet and crew deliver an experience that is equal parts luxury and adventure.

Miami Yacht FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

The most popular Miami yacht charter route takes you from our Biscayne Bay marina south past the Rickenbacker Causeway, around Star Island and the Venetian Islands, then north toward Haulover sandbar. Along the way you will enjoy panoramic views of the Miami skyline, waterfront mansions, and the Port of Miami. For sunset charters, our captains position the yacht west of South Beach for golden-hour views that are truly unmatched. We can also customize routes to include Fisher Island, Key Biscayne, or a run along the South Beach coastline.
We recommend resort-casual attire. Swimsuits and cover-ups are perfect for daytime cruises, and light, breathable clothing works best in Miami's warm climate. Bring a light jacket or wrap for evening charters, as the ocean breeze can cool things down after sunset. Soft-soled shoes or boat shoes are ideal for the deck — we ask that guests avoid hard-soled shoes, stilettos, or dark-soled shoes that can scuff the teak. Sunscreen, sunglasses, and a hat are highly recommended. We provide towels on board.
Absolutely. Star Island is one of the highlights of any Biscayne Bay yacht charter. Our captains cruise slowly past the island and provide commentary on the waterfront estates owned by celebrities, athletes, and business moguls. You will also pass the Venetian Islands, Fisher Island, and Palm Island, each home to some of Miami's most exclusive properties. The views from the water are far superior to any land-based tour, and you can photograph the estates from a unique vantage point without the crowds of bus tours.
Miami sunset charters are our most requested experience. We recommend boarding approximately two hours before sunset so you can enjoy a leisurely cruise through Biscayne Bay before the main event. In winter months, sunset falls around 5:45 PM, while summer sunsets occur closer to 8:15 PM. Our captains position the yacht in the ideal spot — typically west of South Beach or near the mouth of the bay — where the Miami skyline silhouettes against vivid oranges, pinks, and purples. After dark, the city lights reflecting off the water create a second show that is equally stunning.
Yes, anchoring for swimming is one of the best parts of a Miami yacht charter. The most popular spot is the Haulover sandbar, where dozens of boats gather on weekends for a lively sandbar party — you can wade in waist-deep water, enjoy drinks, and socialize. For a quieter swim, our captains can anchor near Key Biscayne or in the calm waters south of the Venetian Islands. All of our yachts are equipped with swim platforms, boarding ladders, and water toys. We provide snorkeling gear on request, and the crew is always on hand to ensure a safe, enjoyable experience.
