Mission Bay & Pacific Coast

Ride the California Coast

San Diego delivers one of the finest jet ski experiences on the West Coast. Launch from Mission Bay — one of the largest man-made aquatic parks in the country — and enjoy miles of calm, protected water perfect for riders of every skill level. The bay's gentle conditions make it an ideal starting point before venturing out to the open Pacific.

For more adventurous riders, the Pacific coastline awaits just beyond the jetty. Cruise past the dramatic La Jolla sea cliffs, where you can spot sea lions basking on the rocks and the occasional pod of dolphins surfing the swells. With over 260 days of sunshine per year, San Diego is a jet ski destination that delivers virtually every day of the year.

San Diego FAQ

Common Questions About Jet Skiing in San Diego

Everything you need to know before your San Diego jet ski rental.

Our San Diego jet ski rentals launch from Mission Bay, one of the largest man-made aquatic parks in the United States. Mission Bay offers over 4,200 acres of calm, protected water — perfect for beginners and families. Experienced riders can head past the jetty into the open Pacific, cruising along the coastline toward La Jolla's dramatic sea cliffs and the famous Cove. The bay and the ocean offer two very different riding experiences within a single rental.
San Diego enjoys year-round jet ski conditions thanks to its mild Mediterranean climate. Summer months (June through September) bring the warmest water temperatures around 68-72 degrees Fahrenheit and the calmest ocean conditions. However, spring and fall are also excellent — fewer crowds, comfortable air temperatures in the 70s, and still-rideable water. Even winter offers surprisingly good days, though morning fog ("June Gloom" can extend into other months) may occasionally delay launch times. We operate 365 days a year, weather permitting.
Yes. Mission Bay connects to the Pacific Ocean through a channel near the jetty. Our Explorer and Odyssey packages include ocean access, allowing you to ride along the San Diego coastline. We require a brief safety orientation before ocean riding, as conditions can differ significantly from the bay — swells, currents, and boat traffic increase once you pass the breakwater. Our team will assess ocean conditions each day and advise riders on the best routes. First-time riders typically start on Mission Bay and work up to ocean riding during their session.
Marine life sightings are one of the highlights of jet skiing in San Diego. Sea lions are commonly spotted near the La Jolla sea caves and along the rocky shoreline — you can often see them from the water as you cruise past. Dolphins are frequently seen in the waters off Mission Beach and Pacific Beach, especially in the morning hours. We always maintain a respectful distance from wildlife as required by federal marine mammal protection guidelines, but it is not unusual to see pods of dolphins riding the same swells as you. Seasonal whale sightings (December through April) are also possible from the water.
