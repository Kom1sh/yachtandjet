About This Location

Explore the Halifax River & Intracoastal Waterway

Daytona Beach is known worldwide for its racing heritage, but the real thrill is on the water. Our Daytona Beach jet ski rental base sits along the Halifax River, part of the scenic Intracoastal Waterway that winds through some of Central Florida's most beautiful natural landscapes. The calm, protected river waters make Daytona one of the most beginner-friendly and affordable jet ski destinations in our network.

Ride south toward the historic Ponce de Leon Inlet Lighthouse, one of the tallest lighthouses in America, and watch for bottlenose dolphins that call the Halifax River home. The Intracoastal Waterway offers miles of uninterrupted riding through mangrove-lined channels and past waterfront homes. Whether you are visiting for Bike Week, a family vacation, or a weekend getaway, a jet ski ride on the Halifax River is the perfect way to experience Daytona Beach from a completely different perspective.

Daytona Beach FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about jet ski rentals in Daytona Beach.

Our Daytona Beach jet ski rentals launch from the Halifax River, which is part of the Intracoastal Waterway. The Halifax River runs between the mainland and the barrier island that Daytona Beach sits on, offering miles of calm, protected water. You can ride north through scenic residential waterways or head south toward the Ponce de Leon Inlet, where the river meets the Atlantic Ocean. The river provides a smooth, enjoyable ride with plenty of scenery and wildlife along the way.
Daytona Beach offers good jet ski conditions from March through November. The peak season runs from April through October when water temperatures are warmest and days are longest. Spring and fall offer the most comfortable temperatures with fewer crowds. Winter riding is possible on milder days, though water temperatures drop to the mid-60s. We operate year-round, weather permitting, and our team will keep you informed about conditions for your chosen date.
Dolphin sightings are one of the highlights of jet skiing on the Halifax River. Atlantic bottlenose dolphins are year-round residents of the Intracoastal Waterway and are frequently spotted during rides, especially in the early morning and late afternoon. It is not uncommon for dolphins to swim alongside your jet ski or play in your wake. While we cannot guarantee sightings, our guides know the areas where dolphins are most often found and will route your ride accordingly.
Daytona Beach is one of the best locations for beginner jet ski riders. The Halifax River offers calm, protected waters with minimal wave action — a stark contrast to open-ocean riding. Our Yamaha WaveRunner EX models feature intelligent throttle control that limits speed until you are comfortable, making them exceptionally easy to operate. Every rental includes a hands-on safety briefing, and our instructors will have you riding confidently within minutes. The flat, calm river conditions mean beginners can focus on enjoying the ride rather than battling waves.
Our standard jet ski rentals operate on the Halifax River and Intracoastal Waterway, not in the open Atlantic Ocean. The river provides a safer, more enjoyable riding experience with calmer water, better wildlife viewing, and stunning scenery. For experienced riders, our Odyssey full-day package can include a supervised ride near the Ponce de Leon Inlet where the river meets the ocean, conditions permitting. Ocean riding requires calm seas and is always at the discretion of our safety team.
